Known as the "father of the atomic bomb," Oppenheimer's leadership and scientific expertise were instrumental in the project's success. After the war, he became a chief advisor to the newly created United States Atomic Energy Commission, where he advocated for international control of nuclear power and opposed the development of the hydrogen bomb. His stance led to political controversy and the revocation of his security clearance during the Red Scare. Despite this, Oppenheimer is remembered as one of the most significant figures in 20th-century science and a symbol of the ethical dilemmas posed by scientific advancement.
